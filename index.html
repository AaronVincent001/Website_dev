WARCRAFT III

Warcraft III is a landmark high-fantasy real-time strategy (RTS) game developed by Blizzard Entertainment. Instead of just commanding massive, faceless armies, the game uniquely blends traditional strategy with role-playing elements. Players harvest resources, build bases, and lead forces across four distinct factions: the noble Humans, the shamanic Orcs, the corrupt Undead, and the reclusive Night Elves.

The Four Factions

The Human Alliance

A resilient coalition of humans, elves, and dwarves. Led by noble Paladins and Archmages, they rely on heavy plate armor, powerful magic, and fortified peasant militias to hold the line.

The Orcish Horde

Fearsome warriors led by Thrall, seeking a new destiny in the barren lands of Kalimdor. They favor brute physical strength, Shamanistic lightning, and the raw power of the Tauren.

The Undead Scourge

A horrifying army of the walking dead, commanded from the frozen north by the Lich King. They blight the very ground they walk upon and raise fallen enemies to bolster their ranks.

The Night Elves

Ancient, shadowy guardians of the forest who strike from the trees. Empowered by the moon goddess Elune, their buildings can uproot and walk, and their archers melt into the shadows at night.

HISTORY

1998: Following the cancellation of the point-and-click adventure game Warcraft Adventures: Lord of the Clans in mid-1998, Blizzard shifts focus toward a true 3D entry in the Warcraft universe.

1999: Blizzard reveals the game at the European Computer Trade Show (ECTS) as a Role-Playing Strategy (RPS) game. It features a tight, over-the-shoulder camera angle focusing on controlling small squads and hero parties rather than managing massive bases.

The Great RTS Pivot (2000): The development team realizes that managing a base and economy is incredibly frustrating with a low-slung, close-up camera. They pull the camera back to a classic isometric viewpoint, turning the project back into an RTS while retaining the innovative "Hero" mechanics.

Art Style Overhaul (2001): Early alpha versions use a dark, gritty, realistic art style, but the units look too small and texturally confusing from a distance. Art Director Samwise Didier pivots the team toward a bold, vibrant, chunky "comic book" aesthetic to improve gameplay readability.

Reign of Chaos Launches (2002): In July 2002, Warcraft III: Reign of Chaos is officially released. It trims down six originally planned factions to four highly distinct, asymmetrical races: Humans, Orcs, the Undead, and the Night Elves.

The Frozen Throne Expansion (2003): Blizzard releases The Frozen Throne expansion pack, introducing the custom map editor tools that would later spawn entire genres (like MOBAs via Defense of the Ancients, or DotA).
